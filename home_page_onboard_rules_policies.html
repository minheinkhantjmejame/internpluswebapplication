GUIDELINES FOR COMPLYING WITH THE PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT

  1. Do not use or disclose personal information without the consent of the owner of personal data. Format of information that the company stores includes: first name, last name, telephone number, address, email address, national ID card number, photograph, work history, age, race, ethnicity, political opinions, religious beliefs, health information, and more. This must comply with the Personal Data Protection Committee guidelines.
  2. It is prohibited to collect personal information about race, ethnicity, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, sexual behavior, criminal history, health information, and other sensitive information without the express consent of the owner of personal data.
  3. It is prohibited to use or disclose personal information for purposes other than the purposes stated while collecting the information.
  4. Requesting consent must be made expressly. This can be in writing or through an electronic system unless such means are not possible.
  5. The personal data collector must collect, use, or disclose personal data only for the purposes informed to the owner before or at the time of collection.
  6. Requesting consent must be clearly separated from other messages, using clear and accessible language.
  7. Collection of personal information must be necessary for legitimate purposes.
  8. It is prohibited to collect personal information from sources other than directly from the owner.
  9. When transferring personal data abroad, the receiving country or organization must have adequate data protection standards in line with Section 16 (5).
